## What is the Document Editor?

The Document Editor is a built-in text editor that opens in a new tab. It provides a clean, distraction-free writing space with all of Helperbird's accessibility features available. You can use dyslexia-friendly fonts, text-to-speech, and other tools while writing.

---

## Who is This For?

This feature is helpful for:

- Students writing essays or assignments
- Anyone who needs a simple, accessible text editor
- People who want to write with dyslexia-friendly fonts
- Writers who benefit from text-to-speech to review their work
- Anyone who wants a clean writing space without distractions

---

## Step 1: Open Helperbird

Click the Helperbird icon in your browser toolbar. It looks like a small owl and is usually in the top-right corner of your browser.

This will open the Helperbird menu.

---

## Step 2: Go to the All Features Section

Scroll down to the bottom of the Helperbird menu. You will see a section called **All Features**.

Click the arrow next to **All Features** to expand it and show all available features.

---

## Step 3: Find Document Editor

Look through the list of features until you see **Document Editor**.

---

## Step 4: Open the Editor

Click the **open** button next to Document Editor.

A new browser tab will open with the Helperbird document editor.

---

## Step 5: Start Writing

The editor opens with a blank document ready for you to write.

You can:

- Type your content directly in the editor
- Use formatting tools to style your text
- Apply Helperbird features like dyslexia fonts and text-to-speech
- Save your work when you are done

The editor provides a simple, clean space for writing without the distractions of a full webpage.

**Tip:** Use the Document Editor when you need to write something and want access to all of Helperbird's reading and writing support tools in one place.
