## What are Keyboard Shortcuts?

Keyboard shortcuts let you quickly activate Helperbird features by pressing key combinations instead of opening the menu and clicking. For example, you could press a few keys to instantly start text-to-speech or turn on Reading Mode.

Shortcuts are disabled by default because some websites have their own keyboard shortcuts that might conflict with Helperbird's.

---


## Who is This For?

This feature is helpful for:

- People who want faster access to features
- Anyone who prefers using the keyboard over the mouse
- Power users who use Helperbird features frequently
- People with motor difficulties who find keyboard navigation easier
- Anyone who wants to be more efficient

---


## Step 1: Open Helperbird

Click the Helperbird icon in your browser toolbar. It looks like a small owl and is usually in the top-right corner of your browser.

This will open the Helperbird menu.

---


## Step 2: Open the Options Menu

Click the **gear icon** in the top-left corner of the Helperbird menu.

This will open the Options panel with various settings and options.

---


## Step 3: Enable Shortcuts

In the Options panel, look for **Shortcuts** with a toggle switch next to it.

Click the toggle switch to turn it **on**. The toggle will turn green when it is enabled.

You can now use keyboard combinations to quickly access Helperbird features.

---


## Step 4: View All Shortcuts

To see all available keyboard shortcuts, click on **Shortcuts** in the Options panel.

A Keyboard shortcuts panel will appear showing all available shortcuts. Each shortcut shows the feature name and its key combination.

Common shortcuts include:

- Turning Helperbird on/off
- Activating Word Prediction
- Opening Reading Mode
- Starting Text to Speech
